Analytics & Reporting

How to Read Google Analytics Reports Without Getting Lost

Traffic reports are only useful if they help you make better decisions. The goal is not to stare at dashboards — it is to understand where people come from, what they do, and whether that activity turns into leads.

Start With Acquisition

Look at where traffic is coming from first. Organic search, paid ads, direct traffic, referrals, and social can all tell a different story. If your local SEO is working, you should see search traffic that aligns with your service areas and target pages.

Watch Behavior, Not Just Visits

Page views alone do not mean much. Look for landing pages, time on page, and the path people take through the site. Are they reaching service pages? Are they getting to contact pages? Are they dropping off before taking action?

Connect Reports to Leads

The most important question is whether traffic is producing calls, forms, or appointments. If a channel brings visitors but no meaningful action, it may not be helping your business. For home service companies, qualified local intent matters more than raw volume.

Use Reports to Improve

Analytics should help you decide where to focus. Improve pages that attract traffic but fail to convert. Double down on channels that generate real leads. Clean up pages that are confusing, slow, or weak on mobile.
